The job

What this actually involves

Being a quant trader at a firm like Optiver isn't just sitting at a desk clicking buttons. The edge lives in three places — and being able to operate across all three is what separates good traders from great ones.

01

Research

The foundation of every edge. Building quantitative models that actually explain price behaviour in index options markets — vol surfaces, skew dynamics, term structure, and how they break down in different regimes.

Good research isn't running backtests until something looks good. It's understanding why a phenomenon exists, whether it's structural or incidental, and whether it will persist. The bar is high because the market is full of people doing the same thing.

02

Systems & Stack

Ideas mean nothing if they can't be executed reliably, at speed, at scale. The systems layer is where research meets reality — order management, pricing engines, risk monitoring, data pipelines, latency optimization.

Being able to build and reason about these systems directly changes what you can trade. The pipeline from idea to live position has to be seamless, and every friction point in it is an opportunity cost.

03

Discretionary Vol Trading

Index options are fundamentally vol products. Beyond running systematic strategies, there's real edge in the ability to read vol markets discretionarily — understanding when implied vol is mispriced relative to realised, when skew is overdone, when the surface is telling you something the models haven't captured yet.

This is where market intuition compounds on top of the quantitative framework. The models give you the foundation; the discretionary layer lets you act on things models don't see.

The actual edge

Why it all connects

Most people are good at one of the three. The real edge is in the feedback loop between them.

Research → Systems

Models only matter if they run

A model that takes 200ms to compute has a different use case than one that runs in 2μs. Being able to build the systems yourself means research is designed with execution in mind from the start — not adapted to it later.

Systems → Trading

Infrastructure is alpha

Better data pipelines mean better signals. Lower latency means tighter spreads. The trading desk that invests in its own stack captures opportunities that disappear by the time they reach slower infrastructure.

Trading → Research

Live markets inform models

No model survives contact with markets unchanged. Discretionary observation of how vol behaves in real conditions is the best feedback loop for improving research. Desk time compounds.

The whole picture

From theory to execution

The abstraction stack runs from pure mathematics through statistical models through code through systems through live markets. Being comfortable at every level of it is genuinely rare — and that's the bet.

The product

Index Options

Index options are one of the most liquid, complex, and intellectually interesting derivatives markets in the world. The surface encodes the market's collective view of future variance, tail risk, and correlation — all simultaneously.
