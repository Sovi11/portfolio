Poker

Variance, reads, and staying rational under uncertainty. It's not a coincidence that traders are drawn to this game.

How I play

Cash games, mostly

Tournaments are exciting. Cash games are where the real work is. The depth of decisions in a cash game — pot geometry, range construction, exploitative vs. GTO lines — is a different beast from tournament poker, where stack dynamics and ICM pressure dominate thinking.

Why cash

Decisions per hour

Cash games run deeper and faster in terms of meaningful spots. You're playing more hands, making more close decisions, and getting real-time feedback on whether your reads and lines are working.

The mental game

Variance tolerance

You can play perfectly and lose for weeks. Managing that — staying process-oriented, not results-oriented — is the same muscle you use in trading. The edge is real; the variance around it is brutal.

What poker teaches

Probabilistic thinking

Every decision is a distribution, not a point. The best play with two aces can still lose to garbage. The habit of thinking in probabilities, not outcomes, is genuinely transferable.

The read

Exploitative play

Game theory gives you a baseline. But most players have exploitable leaks — bet-sizing tells, range imbalances, tilt patterns. Finding and adjusting to those is where the money is at recreational stakes.
